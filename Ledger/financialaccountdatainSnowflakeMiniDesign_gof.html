Overview

The financialaccount service is a primitive accounting model using books service (and spanner) as the source of record for balances and balance changes, while maintaining transaction data in a separate dynamodb instance. We currently have three tenants which use our system: ledger, customerbalances, and cashbacker. We export balance data from books for ledger and customerbalances into Snowflake, but do not export transaction or account data, and we currently do not export any data into Snowflake for cashbacker. This doc focuses on a plan to get all financialaccount data, including accounts, transactions, and balances, into Snowflake.

Audience

  • Accounting Engineering - Payment Balances team

Background & Project Overview

Cash back (“Square Local Offers”) uses financial accounts to track rewards earned and redeemed by a buyer. When a user onboards, an account in financialaccount is created. When cash back is earned and redeemed, transactions are created in financialaccount. The funding for these cash rewards comes partially from Square and partially from merchants, and funds are transferred to buyers via Cash App.

The Customer Engagement team would like the ability to audit money movement, as well as monitor liability (i.e. money owed to customers). This can be achieved by solely exporting balance information to Snowflake from books, however, the CE team has expressed, they would prefer to have the decorative data that comes from transactions and accounts and would wait for a comprehensive solution that exports all FA data into Snowflake.

Goals

  • To establish a path towards all new data from financialaccount service to be available in Snowflake

Non-Goals

  • To backfill existing data into Snowflake (teams have expressly stated this is not needed)

Eng Design

The Data Streaming Platform is implementing Kafka Connector for Snowflake. Once implemented, they will be able to take existing kafka feeds and feed them directly into Snowflake with no work on our side. Aside from a small bug, currently being tracked with Confluent, this solution is almost ready to go. We will wait for this to be implemented and take advantage of the decrease in scope for this project.[b]

Since we already have Kafka feeds for all data written to financialaccount, we only need Kafka Connector to be hooked up, and do not require additional eng-work on our team[c].

Open Issues

There is currently a bug being tracked with Confluent (slack thread here)[f] that is blocking this from moving forward. Once this is resolved, we should be able to request the Data Streaming Platform team to hook up our existing kafka feeds directly to Snowflake.

Alternative Designs

  1. At first, we considered getting data into Snowflake by exporting data first into S3, and then into Snowflake, since the S3 -> Snowflake rails already exist. However, the data streaming platform team is already working on a kafka connector directly into Snowflake. Taking on additional scope didn’t make sense due to the flexible nature of the timeline for this project.
  2. Another alternative was to export first solely books data via dataflow job (the same way we currently export balance data for ledger and customerbalances), and follow up with account and transaction data later in the year, however the Customer Engagement team expressed their timeline is not strict and would prefer to implement a single solution later on.
